📋 Pasted Words — What This Means
Detected content insertions larger than expected typing bursts

How detection works

ProcessAuth monitors the system clipboard every second. When clipboard content exceeds 150 characters, it is classified as a significant paste event. Pasted Words = total pasted characters ÷ 5.

What triggers this count

  • Copying text from a website, PDF, or another document and pasting it in
  • Pasting content from an AI chatbot (ChatGPT, Gemini, etc.)
  • Large block insertions appearing without keystroke build-up
  • Images or URLs copied from web browser

Score impact

  • 150–300 chars → −5 pts
  • 300–600 chars → −10 pts
  • >600 chars → −18 pts
  • URL pasted → −8 pts
  • Image pasted → −12 pts
  • Burst (2 pastes in 3s) → additional −5 pts
⚡
A high pasted-word count is a strong signal of externally sourced content. The actual copied text is shown in the Suspicious Events table below.
